--- layout: page title: About permalink: /about/ priority: 0.9 intro: | Hi, thanks for swinging by! I'm Nicolas Castrillon, but you can call me Nico. My background is a bit like a composite material: the matrix is hands-on mechanical design, and the reinforcing fibers are computational methods for engineering analysis. I am intrigued by human-centered design methodologies and pasionate about innovation. These drivers are a constant in my work. At the [Multiphysics Simulation and Optimization Lab](https://msol.berkeley.edu/) at UC Berkeley, my Ph.D. aids scientists and engineers in industry make sense of novel, complex manufacturing processes with the help of numerical multiphysics simulations, uncertainty quantification, and user-centered design methods. My work has been directly supported by [BASF SE](https://www.basf.com/global/en.html), [Siemens Corp.](https://www.siemens.com/global/en.html), the [Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory](https://www.llnl.gov/), [and Intact Solutions Inc.](https://www.intact-solutions.com/pnp/) Previous to graduate school, I was a mechanical design engineer for laundry appliances at GE Appliances in Louisville, KY and for gas turbine power plants at GE Power in Houston, TX. I am also a co-inventor for a [design patent](https://patents.google.com/patent/US20130232810A1/en). Through design for manufacturability principles, I designed parts and assemblies using advanced additive manufacturing methods and traditional fabrication processes. I love working in teams with diversity, equity, and inclusion. I was a Develop & Inspire Leader at the GE Hispanic Forum in Houston and I am currently the Corporate Liaison for [LAGSES](https://lagses.berkeley.edu/) at Berkeley. Both are roles where I have brought to life events with executives and recruiters to further the careers of minority members in these organizations. Additionally, I am constantly self-educating about my racial biases and redefining my personal intersectionality. I lead a [book club](https://gwe.berkeley.edu/media-club/) spanning LAGSES and the Graduate Women in Engineering (GWE) where students of all walks of life discuss various books and media to spur safe but uncomfortable conversations. In my spare time, I am an avid soccer player, a social rock climber, and an adventurous hiker-dad.
